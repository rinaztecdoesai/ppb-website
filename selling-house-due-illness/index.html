    Can you sell a house if the owner has dementia?

    Yes — in many cases it's possible.

    Whether a home can be sold depends on the owner's mental capacity and the legal arrangements that are in place. Where a valid Lasting Power of Attorney for property and financial affairs exists, the appointed attorneys can usually manage the sale on the owner's behalf.

    Many of the families we help get in touch when a loved one has already moved into care and the home is now empty. If there's no LPA in place, an application to the Court of Protection may be needed — and we're happy to talk through the options with you so you know exactly where you stand. There's no pressure, and no obligation.

    Frequently asked questions

    Selling due to ill health or to fund care? Here’s what families ask us most.

    Can you sell a house to pay for care-home fees?

    Yes. Selling a property is one of the most common ways families release funds to pay for residential or nursing care. A fast cash sale gives you a known sum quickly, and removes the cost of maintaining an empty home while care fees mount up.

    Can I sell my parent's house if they're in care?

    It depends on the legal arrangements in place. Where you hold a valid Lasting Power of Attorney for property and financial affairs, or you are a deputy appointed by the Court of Protection, you may be able to manage the sale on their behalf. We regularly work with families, attorneys and solicitors and can talk you through the options.

    Can a house be sold if the owner has dementia?

    Yes, in many cases — it depends on the owner's mental capacity and the legal arrangements in place. Where a valid Lasting Power of Attorney exists, an attorney can usually manage the sale. Where there is no LPA, an application to the Court of Protection may be needed. We can guide you to the right next step.

    How quickly can you complete?

    We can make a cash offer quickly and complete in as little as 5 days, or on a longer timescale if that suits you better. You choose the completion date that fits your family's circumstances.

    Are there any fees?

    No. There are no estate agency fees and no commissions, we cover your legal fees, and there is no obligation to accept our offer.
