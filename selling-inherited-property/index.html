    What happens when you inherit a house in the UK?

    When you inherit a property, it usually becomes part of the probate process. This means the estate must be legally administered before ownership can fully transfer or the property can be sold (in most cases).

    Once probate is granted, the executor can:

    • Sell the property on the open market
    • Transfer ownership to a beneficiary
    • Sell directly to a cash buyer for speed and simplicity

    We can help at any stage of this process, including before probate is granted (subject to legal process).

    Can you sell an inherited property before probate?

    Yes — in many cases you can start the selling process before probate is completed, and even agree a sale in principle. However:

    • Legal completion usually happens after probate is granted
    • Executors must have authority to sell
    • Solicitors handle final transfer of ownership

    We work closely with probate solicitors to ensure the process runs smoothly and compliantly.
