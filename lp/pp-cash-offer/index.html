    Things every seller should know before you sign anything

    We've spent 30+ years buying property direct from sellers, and we've seen every trick the wider industry uses. This is what we want you to know before you choose anyone, even if you don't choose us.

    1

    Verify they are a real cash buyer

    Many companies that advertise "we buy any house" actually pass your lead to a network of investors. Ask: do you use your own funds? Get the answer in writing.

    ✓ How we're different

    We use only our own funds. No investor network, no broker chain, ever. We can show you proof of funds before any conversation about offers.

    2

    Don't sign anything that ties you in

    Genuine cash buyers do not ask for exclusivity periods, option agreements, or "lock-out" clauses up-front. If they do, walk away.

    ✓ How we're different

    No exclusivity. No lock-outs. No option agreements. You can walk away any time before exchange. No penalties, no fees, no fine print.

    3

    Get the offer fixed in writing

    A real offer doesn't move at exchange. Watch for "subject to survey" tricks that lead to last-minute reductions you can't recover from.

    ✓ How we're different

    What we agree at the start is what we pay at completion. Always. Our offer is fixed. No "subject to" clauses, no last-minute negotiations.

    4

    Check who pays the legal fees

    A direct cash buyer covers your legals. If you're being asked to pay them, you're not selling to a real buyer.

    ✓ How we're different

    We cover all your legal fees. You pay nothing. Not for solicitors, not for surveys, not for completion. Zero deductions from the agreed price.

    5

    Read reviews from sellers who actually completed

    Anyone can post initial offers. Look for stories of completed sales. That's what tells you the buyer follows through.

    ✓ How we're different

    Thousands of verified completions since 1993. We're happy to put you in touch with sellers we've completed with in the past 30 days. Real names, real addresses.

    📋 Your checklist

    5 questions to ask before you sign anything

    Print this list. Ask every cash buyer you speak to. Including us. The honest ones answer all 5 with a straight "yes".

    Q1

    "Do you buy with your own funds?"

    If they hesitate, or say "we have a network of investors", they're a broker, not a buyer.

    Q2

    "Will the offer ever change?"

    A real cash buyer gives you a fixed offer. "Subject to" clauses mean the price can drop at exchange.

    Q3

    "Who pays the legal fees?"

    The buyer should cover yours. If they ask you to pay, you're not really being bought from.

    Q4

    "Will I be tied into anything?"

    You should be free to walk away at any point. Exclusivity periods, lock-out clauses, option agreements = run.

    Q5

    "Can I see proof you've completed before?"

    Reviews of completed sales. Not just initial offers. Recent, verifiable, named sellers where possible.

    ⚠ Red flags

    Warning signs of a fake cash buyer

    If you spot any of these, walk away. We've seen sellers lose months of their lives. And tens of thousands of pounds. To companies running these tactics.

    ⚠

    "High offer to lock you in"

    An above-market offer designed to win you over. Once tied in, the price gets renegotiated down before exchange.

    ⚠

    Long delays before contract

    Genuine cash buyers exchange in days, not months. Delays mean they're shopping your property to investors.

    ⚠

    Surprise "due diligence" reductions

    Last-minute price drops citing surveys, searches, or "structural concerns". When nothing's actually changed.

    ⚠

    Fees buried in the small print

    Sourcing fees, admin fees, "completion fees" deducted from the agreed price. A real buyer takes none of these.

    Will I get less than market value?

    As a business that buys properties we don't pay market value. We buy at a discount that depends on the location and condition of the property. We base our figure on properties already sold in the area and the current market, and we'll often do a drive-by survey at our own cost before we give a firm cash offer. Each property is treated independently and we'll offer the maximum amount we can.

    Are there any fees, charges or hidden costs?

    No. Unlike estate agents there are no fees for valuations, and no other hidden costs. We pay for all your legal fees on the sale of your property. We simply make you a free, no-obligation cash offer. If the offer doesn't meet your needs, you're free to walk away.

    How long does a quick house sale take?

    As a fully funded cash buyer, we typically complete transactions in 5 days. If the property is freehold we can complete within 48 hours (dependent on the speed of your solicitors). Leaseholds can take up to 10 days because of the management pack. We complete on a timescale that suits you, whether that's days, weeks or months.

    How fast will I receive the money?

    The money is transferred as soon as the property sale is agreed and completed, within a time frame that suits you, whether that's 5 days or 5 weeks.

    Will I need a solicitor?

    Yes, you can use a solicitor of your own choice, or we can find a reliable one close to where you're living. We pay for all your legal fees, so you don't have to pay a penny.

    What type of properties do you buy?

    We purchase a wide range of properties for cash: houses, flats, bungalows, ex-Local Authority homes. We buy in any condition, including properties with Japanese Knotweed or subsidence. The only type we don't purchase are retirement properties. Aside from that we're here to make a fair cash offer on almost any home.

    Can I change my mind?

    Of course. There's no obligation to proceed once you've received an offer from us. You can change your mind any time, and if you're feeling unsure we'll happily talk it through.

    Does anybody have to know about the sale?

    No. We offer a discreet and confidential service. The only people who need to know are you, us, and the solicitors.

    My property has been on the market 6+ months. Are you still interested?

    Yes. Unlike estate agents (and online estate agents) we are fully funded. We simply agree a price with you before transferring the funds to a solicitor of your choice.

    Do I need to complete lots of paperwork?

    We never ask our clients to sign anything upfront. You just need to complete the usual legal documents associated with any property sale, which will be dealt with through your solicitors.

    I'm about to be repossessed. Is it too late?

    No, it's not too late. We can help. But please get in touch as soon as possible. The sooner you contact us, the more likely we'll be able to stop the repossession taking place.

    Do I need to tell the repossessors that I'm selling?

    No. You won't need to worry about that. We'll contact them on your behalf.

    I have a poor credit rating. Will you still buy my house?

    Absolutely. Your financial circumstances won't affect the sale.

    How will you use the information I provide?

    We collect information about you to process the sale of your property, manage your account, and to contact you about our services. We may share the information with our trusted sister company UK National Properties Ltd. They help us perform the functions required for the sale, ensuring all data protection policies and procedures are in place.
