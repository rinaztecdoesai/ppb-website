    Common reasons homeowners need to sell

    Financial difficulty rarely has a single cause. Whatever has brought you here, a fast, private cash sale can give you breathing space and a way forward.

    Mortgage arrears

    If you’ve fallen behind on payments, selling lets you clear the balance before the pressure escalates — and helps you preserve any equity you’ve built up.

    Repossession proceedings

    If your lender has begun action, a fast sale can help you avoid the long-term consequences of repossession. The earlier you act, the more options you keep.

    Unmanageable debt

    Credit cards, loans, CCJs or HMRC debts can build faster than income allows. Releasing the equity in your home can clear what you owe and reduce the pressure.

    Divorce or separation

    A clean break and a quick sale can help both parties move on. We make selling after divorce or separation simple, fair and fast.

    Redundancy or loss of income

    When a job ends or income drops, mortgage and household bills can quickly become hard to maintain. Selling gives you certainty while you get back on your feet.

    Landlord financial difficulties

    Rising interest rates, rent arrears and rising running costs can turn a rental into a burden. We buy tenanted and vacant properties for cash, fast.

    Concerned about repossession?

    If proceedings have already started, you may have more options than you think.

    Can I sell if repossession has started? In many cases, yes. Lenders will often allow a sale to go ahead because it repays the mortgage in full without them having to complete the repossession — which is usually a better outcome for everyone. The earlier you act, the more flexibility you have to agree a sale and protect any equity.

    If your lender has issued letters, a court date or a possession order, time matters. A direct cash sale can complete in days, giving you a clear way to settle the mortgage and step away with certainty. You can read more on our repossession page.

    Frequently asked questions

    In financial difficulty? Here’s what people ask us most.

    Can I sell if I'm in mortgage arrears?

    Yes. Being in arrears does not stop you selling. A fast sale repays your mortgage from the proceeds, clears the arrears and stops the pressure escalating — and acting early usually gives you more options.

    Can I sell if repossession has started?

    In many cases, yes. Lenders will often allow a sale because it repays the mortgage in full without them having to complete repossession. The earlier you act, the more flexibility you have.

    Will I keep any equity?

    If your property is worth more than your mortgage balance and any secured debts, you keep the remaining equity after completion. We’ll be clear about the figures from the start.

    How fast can you buy?

    We give a free, no-obligation offer quickly and can complete in days when speed matters. You choose a completion date that suits your situation.

    Are there any fees?

    No estate agent fees, no commissions and no hidden charges. In many cases we cover your legal fees too. The offer we agree is the amount you receive.
